About Us

Kianyaga High School is a national boys' school in Kirinyaga County, known for excellence in academics and discipline. Founded in 1961, it follows the motto "Enter to Excel, Exit to Prosper."

  1. Home
  2. About Us

A brief History about our School

Kianyaga High School was established in the 1960s through community efforts in Gichugu Constituency, Kirinyaga County, Kenya. It started as a harambee (self-help) project, spearheaded by local elders who wanted to create an advanced learning institution in the area. Initially, the school faced tough conditions, earning the nickname "Kalahari" due to its harsh environment, including mud-walled dormitories that were later upgraded in the 1980s. Over the years, the school has grown into a national boys' school, known for academic excellence and discipline. Many notable figures, including Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua and former NYS Director-General Dr. Nelson Githinji, are proud alumni. Guided by its motto, "Enter to Excel, Exit to Prosper," Kianyaga High continues to produce successful graduates who impact society positively.
